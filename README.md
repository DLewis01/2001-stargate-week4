# 2001-stargate-week4
A digital recreation of the 2001:A space Odyssey stargate


A milestone quietly past on Sunday. It was Keir Dullea's 90th birthday. You probably know him as Dave Bowman from 2001 a space odessey. So for this weeks code I decided to do a tribute for his birthday.
This is the stargate scene from one of the greatest movies ever made. The original was made by Douglas Trumbull using a technology called slit scan. Before his untimely death I chatted with him and he explained how he painted with light on celluoid. I was especially interested in his stories about filming Silent Running. 

Douglas Trumbull, then only 25, had already been working on the film for a couple of years before he hit on the idea of streak photography. 

A camera was mounted on a special tracking dolly driven by a worm gear to ensure precise movement. Exposing a single frame it would creep towards a large black screen with a slit in it. Behind the slit was a piece of backlit artwork mounted on a carriage that could move perpendicularly to the camera. This artwork – an abstract painting or a photo blow-up of flowers or coral slid slowly to the right or left as the camera tracked towards it. Remember, this was all just to capture one frame. Repeat this for 24 frames a second for 10 minutes. Painstaking work.

 
Here is my version using css to slide textures. Oh, and before anyone get upset about 'stolen music', the music is streamed off youtube, so yell at Google, not me.
